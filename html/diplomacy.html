Overview

Diplomacy is a strategic board game created by Allan B. Calhamer in 1954. Despite its long play times and complicated interplay, the rules of Diplomacy are surprisingly simple. There is no luck in this game of pure negotiation and strategy

Gameplay

In Diplomacy, players represent one of the seven great powers in Europe in the years leading up to World War I. The object is to control the majority (18) of the supply centers in Europe, using your own units to do so. However, no single nation has the power to take over Europe, so players

must work with other nations in order to succeed.

Strategies

While Diplomacy's rules are fairly simple, its strategies are not. Here are a few important strategies:

  1. Forming and breaking alliances strategically
    • Alliances are the crux of this game
    • You will have the chance, every round, to negotiate with other players at the table
    • You need to carefully and secretly set up alliances to go to war against some countries
    • However, there is no guarantee that a country will be honest, or keep any deals they make with you
  2. Controlling key supply centers
    • Supply centers in the "center" of the board, especially near Austria, are incredibly important
    • If no single country can gain control of these central areas, it is likely that the game will end in stalemate
    • You may need to make unlilkely alliances and deals to break through the center of the board
  3. Using tactics such as "support" and "convoy" effectively
    • The options you have to move and use troops are incredibly limited
    • Versatile moves such as support are critical to your strategy
    • Supporting a movement or defended area can greatly increase your chances of success and keep your borders strong
    • Well placed convoys, on the other hand, can create surprise reinforcements that take enemies by surprise
    • Use every resource given to you in this game
